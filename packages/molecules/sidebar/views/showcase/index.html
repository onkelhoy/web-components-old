Nav group Nav-groups consist of a nav-heading and multiple nav-items. All nav-items are organized into nav-groups. Nav-groups may or may not have a heading. If a nav-group has a heading, the group can be opened or closed by using the chevron button or by clicking anywhere on the heading. Nav-groups cannot be opened or closed when the sidebar is in the collapsed state.

Behaviour
Truncation Nav-items are truncated with an ellipsis when they overflow their container.
Scrolling Navigation sidebars can be vertically scrolled independent of the rest of the content on the screen in cases where the sidebar’s contents exceed the height of the viewport.
Visibility Navigation sidebars can be open and fully visible or collapsed. The menu icon is used to open and collapse the drawer. Navigation sidebars should be open by default on large screens and closed by default on small screens.

Expand on hover While the navigation sidebar is collapsed, hovering the mouse over the sidebar will cause it to expand on top of page content, called the expanded-hover state. Moving the mouse away from the sidebar will return the sidebar to the collapsed state. Clicking the menu button when the sidebar is in the expanded-hover state will fix the sidebar and it will no longer collapse when the mouse is moved away from the sidebar. Page content will then move to be beside the sidebar instead of underneath it. When in the expanded-hover state, the sidebar has a shadow using the token --pap-shadow-m.
Moving the mouse into the sidebar causes the sidebar to pop-out on top of page content

Contact section The navigation sidebar may have a contact section pinned to the bottom of the sidebar. If there is overlap, the contact section appears on top of the nav-groups.
Nav item states Default, hover, and pressed nav-items use their respective states and use the outlined icon. Active nav-items will use the --pap-color-bg-tertiary token, and swap the outlined icon for a filled icon. Hover, pressed, and active states use the active variant of the badge.

Nav heading states Badges adjust their width depending on their value and have a minimum width of 32px. Only 3 numerical digits including a decimal are supported. After 999, an additional abbreviation for large values is added. “k” for thousand, “m” for million, “bn” for billion, and “tn” for trillion. Translations of these abbreviations are handled on a project basis.
Animation The animation of the navigation sidebar expanding and collapsing and the nav-group opening and closing follows these animation inputs.
Duration: 375ms Stiffness: 400 Damping: 32 Mass: 1
Responsive width The navigation sidebar has a 320px width by default. On screens smaller than 720px, the navigation sidebar is displayed on top of page content and is expanded with the menu button. The sidebar will assume 85% of the viewport width up to a max width of 320px. The max width takes affect at approx 377px viewport width.

Overlay When the navigation sidebar is expanded on mobile and pad, an overlay appears between the navigation drawer and the other content on the page. The overlay uses the color token --pap-color-bg-overlay at 70% opacity.
